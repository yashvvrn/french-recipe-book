Ratatouille

60 minutes
Main Course
Serves 4

Ratatouille is a vibrant vegetable stew with zucchini, eggplant, bell peppers, and tomatoes, simmered in herbs de Provence. This colorful Provençal dish celebrates the bounty of summer vegetables and is as nutritious as it is delicious. Perfect as a main course for vegetarians or as a side dish for meat lovers.

Ingredients

  • 1 large eggplant, cut into 1/4-inch slices
  • 2 zucchini, cut into 1/4-inch slices
  • 2 yellow squash, cut into 1/4-inch slices
  • 2 red bell peppers, seeded and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped
  • 2 teaspoons herbs de Provence
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Preparation

Ratatouille is a celebration of Mediterranean vegetables, layered and baked to perfection. This dish can be prepared in various styles - from rustic and chunky to elegantly arranged. Our version combines both methods, creating a flavorful base sauce with a beautiful arrangement of vegetables on top.

Chef's Tips

  • For the best flavor, use fresh, in-season vegetables.
  • Salting the eggplant before cooking helps remove bitterness.
  • The dish tastes even better the next day after the flavors have melded.
  • Serve warm or at room temperature with crusty bread or over polenta.

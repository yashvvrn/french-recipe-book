French Onion Soup

60 minutes
Appetizer
Serves 4

French Onion Soup is a timeless classic featuring caramelized onions simmered in a rich beef broth, topped with crusty bread and melted Gruyère cheese. This hearty appetizer combines sweet, savory, and umami flavors in a comforting bowl that's perfect for starting a French meal or enjoying on a cold evening.

Ingredients

  • 4 large yellow onions, thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 6 cups beef stock
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 1 baguette, sliced into rounds
  • 2 cups grated Gruyère cheese
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon cognac (optional)

Preparation

This classic French soup requires patience to properly caramelize the onions, which is the key to its deep, rich flavor. The slow cooking process transforms simple ingredients into a luxurious appetizer that's well worth the effort.

Chef's Tips

  • Take your time caramelizing the onions - this is where the flavor develops.
  • Use homemade beef stock if possible for the richest flavor.
  • Traditional Gruyère cheese works best, but Emmental or Swiss can substitute.
  • Toast the baguette slices before adding them to the soup to prevent sogginess.
